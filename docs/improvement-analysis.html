Improvement Analysis · July 1, 2026

Making Meeterz great

Where v1 stands, what's genuinely missing, and a prioritized path — based on the shipped codebase, the category leaders (Granola, Otter, superwhisper, MacWhisper), and the open questions from the original research.

The one-paragraph verdict

v1 nailed the hard technical problem (driver-free dual-channel capture + on-device multilingual transcription) and the core UX shape. What separates it from a product you'd rely on daily is, in order: trust (crash recovery, delete confirmations, a validated real-Teams run), intelligence (summaries and action items — the single feature that made Granola a category leader), and ambient presence (menu-bar recording, calendar awareness). Polish before features; intelligence before ambience.

Tier 1 — Trust & robustness do first

Bugs-in-waiting and friction I can point to in the current code. Cheap to fix, and any one of them failing during a real client meeting destroys trust in the whole app.

Crash recovery for in-flight recordings

~half day

If the app crashes or is force-quit mid-meeting, the meeting is stuck in "recording" forever and the WAV header says 0 bytes — the audio is on disk but unplayable and untranscribed.

On startup: find meetings with status recording, repair WAV headers from file size (the data is intact — WavWriter streams to disk), set real duration, and offer to transcribe. This turns the worst failure mode into a non-event.

Validate against a real Teams call

15 min, manual

The research flagged one report of Core Audio taps failing on Teams specifically (pasrom/meeting-transcriber#79). Our E2E proves loopback works for system TTS — a real Teams call is the one untested variable left.

Join a Teams echo/test call, record 2 minutes, confirm the transcript. If it fails, the fallback is already scoped: a ~200-line Swift helper using a system-wide tap (AudioCap as reference).

Confirm destructive actions + undo

~2 h

Deleting a meeting or folder is currently instant and irreversible — one misclick erases a client meeting's audio, transcript and notes.

macOS-native confirm sheet on delete; move audio to Trash (shell.trashItem) instead of leaving orphans; delete also currently leaves the WAVs on disk — clean that up too.

Pause / resume recording

~half day

Real meetings have breaks and sidebars you don't want recorded. The HUD has Stop only.

Suspend the worklet pipes (keep streams alive), show a paused state in the HUD. The spec's Notion reference has this pattern already (Pause / Stop).

Surface transcription errors with a retry

~2 h

On failure the meeting just shows an "error" state; the actual message goes to the main-process console where nobody sees it, and there's no way to re-run without re-recording.

Store the error message on the meeting, show it in the rail with a "Try again" button (the WAVs are still there — retry is free).

Folder rename UI + drag-and-drop filing

~half day

The rename API exists in main and preload but has no UI — folders can only be created or deleted. Moving a meeting requires opening it and using the dropdown.

Double-click to rename (Finder-style), drag meeting rows onto folders in the sidebar.

Tier 2 — Intelligence the differentiator

What made Granola a breakout wasn't recording — it was what happens after. This tier is where Meeterz goes from "recorder" to "why would I ever take manual minutes again".

AI summaries, action items & decisions

~2–3 days

The highest-value feature in the entire space. After transcription, generate: TL;DR, action items (with owners when inferable), decisions, open questions — blended with your typed notes, Granola-style ("enhance my notes", not "replace my notes").

Two backends, user's choice: local via Ollama (llama3.x / qwen — keeps the 100%-private story intact) or Claude API for best quality as an explicit opt-in. Works in NL/FR/EN since the transcript language is known. Prompt template per meeting type (see templates below).

Full-text search that actually searches

~1 day

Current search is a client-side includes() over raw JSON strings — fine at 20 meetings, useless at 500, and it can't show where the match was.

SQLite FTS5 over title + notes + transcript text, with snippet highlights in results and jump-to-segment in the transcript. This is the feature that makes the archive compound in value over months.

Click-to-seek playback synced with the transcript

~1 day

Segments have timestamps and the audio is right there, but they're not connected — verifying "did she really say X?" means scrubbing manually.

Click a segment → audio jumps there; highlight the current segment during playback; one shared player instead of two stacked ones (pick channel by segment source).

Speaker diarization within a channel

~2–3 days

"Them" is one label for everyone on the Teams call, and the mic channel lumps everyone in the room together. Real meetings have 3+ voices.

Post-meeting pyannote via sherpa-onnx (research-verified route for Electron) on each channel → "Speaker 1/2/3" labels you can rename once and have stick per meeting. This was an open question in the research; sherpa-onnx is the pragmatic answer.

Notes templates + a real editor

~1–2 days

A plain textarea undersells the "notes-first" promise. 1:1s, standups, client calls and interviews have repeating structure.

Tiptap editor (headings, checklists, bold) with 3–4 starter templates; checklist items double as action items the AI can pre-fill. Keep it deliberately minimal — this is not Notion.

Export & share

~half day

Meeting → Markdown / PDF (title, notes, summary, transcript) via Cmd+E; copy-as-Markdown for pasting into Slack/Notion/email. Local-first apps live or die on how easily data gets out.

Tier 3 — Ambient presence the daily-habit layer

Features that remove the "remember to open the app" problem — the reason people abandon meeting recorders after two weeks.

Menu-bar mode + global hotkey

~1–2 days

Tray icon with one-click record (visible red state while recording), ⌥⌘R from anywhere, dock badge during recording. The main window becomes something you open to review, not to start.

Calendar awareness

~2 days

Read the macOS calendar (EventKit via a tiny Swift helper): auto-title meetings from the current event, attach attendees, and nudge — "Weekly sync started. Record?" This is Granola's retention engine and it's very achievable locally.

Live transcript during the meeting

~3–5 days

You chose post-meeting (rightly, for v1) — but streaming whisper.cpp on 15–30 s chunks during recording gets 90% of the live feel with the same stack, feeding the transcript rail in near-real-time. Big demo-wow, useful for "what did he just say?".

Auto-stop on silence + audio hygiene

~1 day

Detect long trailing silence and offer to stop (forgot-to-stop is the #1 recorder failure); mic level check before recording starts ("your mic looks muted"); VAD pre-filter before whisper to skip silent stretches — big speedup on hour-long meetings.

Two things I'd explicitly not do: cloud sync (breaks the local-first promise that differentiates Meeterz; revisit only with E2E encryption) and a meeting-bot that joins calls (Granola's whole thesis — validated by its success — is that bot-free capture is the better UX).
